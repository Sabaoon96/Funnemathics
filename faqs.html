FAQs

  • Who can receive tutoring?
    •
    All those needing any help or are interested in studying math or physics; whether it be Cambridge, NCEA, IB or even help with math & physics at university.

  • What are the tutoring hours and locations?
    •
    Tutoring hours start from 3:30pm to 8:30pm generally. For more information please visit the 'timetable' and 'contact us' pages.

  • Do I have to pay to get tutoring help?
    •
    Yes, all tutoring sessions will have to be paid for.

  • What subjects can I get help with?
    •
    You can get help with any math subject, be it calculus or statistics, as well as physics.

  • How often may I be tutored in a certain subject?
    •
    The tutorial hours and sessions are upto you. You could be tutored either once a week or even daily, depending upon what best suits your needs.

  • Do I have to make an appointment? If I do, how?
    •
    Yes, you will have to contact the tutor before starting the tutorial sessions. You could contact him by email or even call him on his mobile number. Contact details are available on the 'contact us' page.

  • I've never been tutored before. How do I prepare for a tutoring session?
    •
    No special preparation is needed before starting a tutorial session. However, we would recommend the student to bring his/her problems along with themselves, so that they already have something to work on.

  • Who will be helping me?
    •
    The tutor, Shah, will be teaching and helping you throughout the course of the tutorial sessions.

  • Can I get tutoring help online, by email, or by phone?
    •
    This advantage will only be available to those currently studying with the tutor.

  • What happens if I'm sick and can't make it?
    •
    You would firstly need to contact the tutor and let him know of it. Then the tutorial session may be changed and a different session could be allocated to the student.
